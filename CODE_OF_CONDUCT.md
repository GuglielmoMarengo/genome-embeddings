# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Genome Embeddings is committed to providing a respectful, constructive and welcoming environment for everyone who participates in the project.

Participation should be possible regardless of experience level, background, identity, nationality, language, education, disability, appearance, age, religion, sexual orientation, gender identity or other personal characteristics.

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

* communicate respectfully and professionally;
* welcome questions and reasonable disagreement;
* provide constructive, technically grounded feedback;
* focus criticism on ideas, code and evidence rather than individuals;
* acknowledge mistakes and correct them;
* respect different levels of technical and scientific experience;
* avoid overstating biological, medical or clinical conclusions;
* protect private, sensitive and proprietary information;
* comply with repository security and contribution guidelines;
* help maintain an environment suitable for open scientific collaboration.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

* harassment, intimidation or discrimination;
* personal attacks, insults or hostile language;
* threats or encouragement of harm;
* sexualized language or unwanted sexual attention;
* deliberate disruption of discussions or reviews;
* repeated bad-faith arguments;
* publishing another person's private information without permission;
* knowingly submitting malicious code, credentials or harmful files;
* pressuring others to disclose private genomic, clinical or personal data;
* presenting unsupported scientific or clinical claims as established facts;
* retaliation against someone who reports a concern;
* any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional open-source environment.

## Scientific and Technical Discussion

Disagreement is expected in a research-oriented project.

Participants may challenge:

* mathematical definitions;
* normalization choices;
* biological interpretations;
* software architecture;
* test design;
* statistical assumptions;
* roadmap priorities.

Such disagreement must remain evidence-based and respectful.

Strong criticism of an idea is acceptable. Attacking the person proposing it is not.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

* issues;
* pull requests;
* code reviews;
* discussions;
* repository comments;
* project documentation;
* other spaces officially associated with Genome Embeddings;
* public situations where someone is representing the project.

## Enforcement

The project maintainer is responsible for interpreting and enforcing this Code of Conduct.

The maintainer may:

* request that inappropriate content be edited or removed;
* issue a private or public warning;
* close or lock discussions;
* reject contributions;
* temporarily restrict participation;
* permanently ban participation in serious or repeated cases.

Enforcement decisions should be proportionate to the behavior, its impact and whether it is repeated.

## Reporting Conduct Concerns

Reports should be made privately whenever possible.

Contact the project maintainer using the contact information published on the maintainer's GitHub profile.

A report should include:

* what occurred;
* where it occurred;
* relevant links or screenshots;
* who was involved;
* whether the behavior is ongoing;
* any immediate safety or privacy concern.

Reports will be reviewed as confidentially as reasonably possible.

Do not use public issues to disclose sensitive personal information.

Security vulnerabilities should be reported according to `SECURITY.md`, not through the conduct-reporting process.

## Good-Faith Reporting

Reports made honestly and in good faith will not result in retaliation.

Knowingly false, misleading or malicious reports may themselves violate this Code of Conduct.

## Appeals

A participant affected by an enforcement action may request reconsideration by providing relevant context or evidence to the maintainer.

Reconsideration does not guarantee reversal of the original decision.
